// THE STORY BEHIND THE MOVEMENT

DISABLED KIDS RISE UP. CHANGE ALL.

This is the story of why O.V.E.R exists — and why a 10 year old kid decided to build a worldwide movement instead of waiting for someone else to do it.

// CHAPTER ONE

DISABLED KIDS MATTER.

Disabled kids wake up every day knowing the world doesn't always treat them the same as everyone else. They walk into schools, onto playgrounds, into classrooms — and sometimes the world makes them feel like they are less than. Less important. Less worthy. Less deserving of respect.

That is wrong. And Oliver Vo decided he wasn't going to just accept it.

Oliver has Tourette syndrome. He knows what it's like to be different. He knows what it's like to have a body that sometimes does things you can't control. And he knows that disabled kids — no matter what their disability is — deserve to feel like they matter. Like they belong. Like the world is on their side.

I didn't build O.V.E.R because someone told me to. I built it because I looked around and decided disabled kids should feel like they matter — and nobody else was doing it the way it needed to be done.

— OLIVER VO, FOUNDER OF O.V.E.R, AGE 10
// CHAPTER TWO

THE DECISION.

Most people see a problem and wait. They wait for a teacher to fix it. They wait for a law to change. They wait for someone older, bigger, or more important to step up.

Oliver didn't wait.

At 10 years old, living in Ladera Ranch, California, Oliver sat down and decided to build something real. Not a school project. Not a poster for the hallway. A worldwide movement — with a website, care packages, school programs, an ally pledge, and a goal to reach every disabled kid on earth who needs to hear that they are not alone.

He called it O.V.E.R — Oliver Vo's Empowering Real Disabled Kids. Because that's exactly what it does.

// CHAPTER THREE

WHAT O.V.E.R DOES.

O.V.E.R sends care packages to disabled kids around the world — filled with a personal letter, an official membership card, a power journal, O.V.E.R gear, and the simple, powerful message: you matter.

O.V.E.R enters schools and runs assemblies that change how every student treats disabled kids — permanently. Students take the Ally Pledge, promising to never mock, never stay silent, and always stand beside disabled kids.

O.V.E.R puts disabled kids in the spotlight — telling their stories to the world and showing everyone what disabled kids are capable of when the world gets out of their way.

And O.V.E.R is just getting started.

DISABLED KIDS RISE UP.

This is not a charity. This is not pity. This is a movement. Disabled kids are not less — they are powerful, capable, and world-changing. O.V.E.R exists to make sure every disabled kid on earth knows that. And to make sure the world knows it too.
